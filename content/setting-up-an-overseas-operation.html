Setting up an overseas operation

Most firms only set up an overseas operation after testing the market. This can be a logical progression from working with an agent or distributor as a company grows its sales.

The advantages can be attractive and include:

  • lower costs
  • increased market penetration
  • a positive message to the local market
  • improved customer service

Your operation may be owned by your existing company or can be set up with a different owner.

Remember that the learning curve on this route may be steep. Initial costs are likely to be high and there’s more exposure to risk, such as legal liability. This can be offset to an extent by research and planning, and a thorough understanding of the country’s legal and regulatory framework.

See our exporting country guides as a starting point.

Setting up an overseas operation involves some form of capital outlay and the main options are:

  • a local representative office in the export market
  • a joint venture with a company local to the foreign market
  • acquisition of an existing company in the market
  • setting up a subsidiary of a parent UK company, or a standalone company

Get support and advice on getting your product or service to market from a trade adviser in your area.
