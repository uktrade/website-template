Clear goals for face-to-face meetings

It’s important to make sure you’ve thought about clear goals for your face-to-face meetings as they will:

  • feed into a potential agenda or checklist for both sides
  • reassure the person you’re meeting as to what you’re going to cover (this can help them to do any preparation they need to and select an appropriate interpreter, if necessary)
  • give a clear structure to your meeting
  • ensure you get the most out of your time and the best out of the person you’re meeting with

A face-to-face meeting can be as formal or informal as you want to make it and this will often depend on the place you’re visiting and the person you’re going to be talking to. However informal it is, though, knowing what you need to get out of the meeting will help you shape what happens and ensure its success.

Your goals will need to be tailored around the specifics of your particular business, but you could think about:

  • whether you’re just aiming to make contact, or are trying to get to a definite outcome or decision
  • whether it’s just a general information-gathering exercise, or there are more detailed facts and figures you need from the person you’re meeting
  • what you would need to get from the meeting to consider it a success – increased sales, new leads, a better idea of the networks in the area
  • what you can achieve in person that you wouldn’t be able to remotely, like being introduced to other key employees or partners, and seeing offices or warehouses or other locations

To help you get started or increase your success in a new country, consider attending a trade mission arranged either by DIT or another organisation, such as a trade association, the British Chambers of Commerce or Enterprise Nation. The missions take UK businesses on short trips to get on-the-ground experience of a particular country and its business culture and opportunities.
