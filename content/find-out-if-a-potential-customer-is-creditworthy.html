Find out if a potential customer is creditworthy

As with domestic customers,  you need to be clear what the possibility is that you won’t get paid.  By checking your potential customers’ finances, you can make decisions on how much risk your business is prepared to take.  This should be part of your market and business research.

If you’re travelling to your new market, do try to visit potential customers, particularly if they are going to be of high value to your business.

UKEF, your bank or accountant, or companies like Experian can help you do international credit checks on customers.

Other ways to understand whether a customer is creditworthy include:

  • ask them for a bank reference
  • check the country equivalents of Companies House if businesses are required to publish their accounts in that market
  • check their websites and get names of their suppliers and contact them directly or ask the customer if they have suppliers who are willing to give them a reference
  • talk to local Chambers of Commerce and the appropriate industry and country trade associations to see if they are aware of the customer
  • you can also agree to have the first few invoices paid immediately in full to build trust and then make a decision about future credit

If you’re still worried about getting paid, consider getting credit insurance.
